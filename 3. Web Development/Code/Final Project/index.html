Hi, I'm Joe Biden!

I am Joseph R. Biden Jr., currently serving as the 46th President of the United States, a role I assumed in January 2021. My career in public service is extensive, having served as Vice President from 2009 to 2017, where I played a key role in addressing significant national and international challenges. Prior to that, I represented Delaware in the U.S. Senate from 1973 to 2009, where I was recognized for my work on foreign relations, criminal justice, and environmental issues. Affordable Care Act (ACA)

  • As Vice President, Joe Biden played a significant role in advocating for and the passage of the ACA, also known as Obamacare, in 2010, aiming to expand healthcare access and reduce costs for Americans.

American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009

  • Biden oversaw the implementation of this stimulus package designed to help the U.S. economy recover from the Great Recession, focusing on job creation, investing in infrastructure, education, and renewable energy.

Violence Against Women Act (VAWA)

  • As a Senator, Biden authored and helped pass the VAWA in 1994, which provided more resources for the prevention and prosecution of violence against women, including domestic violence and sexual assault, and established the Office on Violence Against Women within the Department of Justice.
